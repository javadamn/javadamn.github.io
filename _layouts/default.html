Javad Aminian-Dehkordi

Part scientist, part builder, always curious.

I am a PhD student in Applied SCience & Technology at UC Berkeley's School of Engineering, where I work at the intersection of computational biology, systems science, and ML.

My central drive is to answer: How can we use computation to truly understand life? I enjoy tackling this question by developing ideas that connect theory with real-world impact—whether through optimization, graph models, or language-based AI systems, particularly for personalized healthcare solutions.

Alongside my core resaerch, my approach is also informed by an interest in how microeconomics concepts can guide our understanding of biological systems. This interdisciplinary perspective was further sharpened through my experience teaching Microeconomic Analysis for Business Decisions at Berkeley’s Haas School of Business—which definitely enriched my broader approach to systems thinking across diverse fields.
